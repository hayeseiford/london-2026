LONDON 2026 TRIP APP — HOW TO GET IT ON BOTH PHONES
=====================================================

The app is a single self-contained file: index.html
Everything (data, styles, scripts) lives inside it. No internet required after first load.

EASIEST OPTION — Netlify Drop (30 seconds, free, no signup needed)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open https://app.netlify.com/drop in your browser.
2. Drag this folder ("London Trip App") onto the page.
3. Netlify gives you a URL like https://random-name-12345.netlify.app
4. Text that URL to Kayla. Done.

To make it feel like a real app on iPhone:
   Open the URL in Safari → Share button → "Add to Home Screen"
   The icon (gold "L" on navy) will appear on your home screen.

On Android:
   Open the URL in Chrome → Menu → "Add to Home screen"


ALTERNATIVE — Dropbox shared link
---------------------------------
This file is already inside Dropbox. To share via Dropbox:
1. Right-click "index.html" in Dropbox → Share → Create link
2. Send the link to Kayla. The catch: Dropbox sometimes shows it as a download
   instead of opening it. To force it to open as a webpage, replace
       ?dl=0
   at the end of the URL with
       ?raw=1

   Even with that, "Add to Home Screen" works less cleanly than Netlify.


ALTERNATIVE — GitHub Pages, Cloudflare Pages, Vercel
-----------------------------------------------------
Any free static host. All work the same way: upload index.html, get a URL.


WHAT THE APP HAS
----------------
• Today tab — countdown, "up next" widget, full overview
• Itinerary tab — every day, every reservation, every entry time
• Hotel tab — Four Seasons contact buttons + travel/transfer summary
• Refs tab — all confirmation codes (tap any to copy) + tap-to-call phone book

Every phone number is tappable to call. Every address opens in Maps.
The countdown auto-updates. Confirmation codes copy to clipboard with a tap.
